# Texas and Regional Grant Programs for CECREH

Texas-specific, Gulf Coast-focused, and nationally competitive funding sources aligned with CECREH's mission at Texas Tech University.

---

## Tier 1: Strongest Fit

### Gulf Research Program (National Academies of Sciences)

- **What it funds:** Health, resilience, and compounding disasters in the Gulf of Mexico region; environment and changing coastlines
- **Award size:** Typically $500K--$2M per project (recent rounds totaled $8.6M across 11 projects)
- **Status:** Active, with periodic open calls
- **Requirements:** Must focus on Gulf of Mexico region; emphasis on equity, community resilience, and evidence-based decision-making
- **CECREH fit:** Excellent. The program recently awarded $8.6M specifically for "equity in health and community resilience for at-risk communities" -- almost exactly CECREH's work. The Gulf Coast tenant vulnerability research, AI disaster guidance for underserved populations, and NWS integration all align directly.
- **Framing:** Position the AI Disaster Agent as a tool for reducing health and housing disparities in disaster outcomes, with the Gulf Hazard Interview data as evidence of community need. CECREH's housing resilience focus adds a dimension most proposals lack.

### Texas GLO Coastal Management Program (Cycle 32)

- **What it funds:** Coastal management projects including community resilience and hazard response planning
- **Award size:** Varies by project type
- **Status:** Applications open March 1, 2026; due June 3, 2026
- **Requirements:** Must benefit the Texas coastal zone
- **CECREH fit:** Strong. Disaster guidance for hurricane and flood-prone coastal communities (Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi) aligns with coastal resilience priorities. The renter vulnerability focus adds an equity dimension. CECREH's housing resilience mission is a natural complement.
- **Framing:** AI-powered coastal hazard guidance system serving vulnerable populations -- particularly renters -- in the Texas coastal zone.
- **Action:** This is the most time-sensitive opportunity. Due June 3, 2026.

### FEMA BRIC (Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities) -- Through TDEM

- **What it funds:** Pre-disaster hazard mitigation, including capability/capacity-building activities and technology
- **Award size:** Capability-building projects typically $1M--$5M; large infrastructure up to $50M
- **Status:** Legally contested but court-ordered to continue. A federal judge issued a permanent injunction in December 2025. Monitor for the reopened FY2024 NOFO.
- **Requirements:** 75/25 cost share (90/10 for small/impoverished communities); sub-applications go through TDEM
- **CECREH fit:** Strong. BRIC's capability-building category funds technology development, planning tools, and community preparedness systems. An AI guidance system integrated with NWS is a pre-disaster preparedness tool. The reduced cost share for impoverished communities aligns with CECREH's vulnerable population focus.
- **Framing:** Non-structural mitigation measure: AI-powered public guidance system for pre-disaster preparedness in vulnerable Texas communities, with emphasis on housing-insecure populations.

### Texas GLO CDBG-DR (2024 Disasters)

- **What it funds:** Disaster recovery and mitigation for Hurricane Beryl and May 2024 derecho
- **Award size:** $555.7M allocated to Texas; Houston received $314M; 15% must go to mitigation
- **Status:** Active; action plans under development
- **Requirements:** Must address unmet needs from 2024 disasters; local government is typically the sub-recipient
- **CECREH fit:** Strong. The required 15% mitigation set-aside could fund an AI guidance system as a forward-looking mitigation investment. The renter vulnerability research aligns with HUD's housing equity mandate. Houston's large allocation and massive renter population make this particularly relevant. CECREH's housing focus is a direct match for HUD's priorities.
- **Framing:** AI-powered mitigation tool that addresses demonstrated information access gaps among renters and vulnerable populations during Hurricane Beryl. CECREH's capacity-building mission provides the institutional framework for sustainable deployment.

## Tier 2: Strong Fit

### EPA Healthy and Resilient Gulf of Mexico Grant Program

- **What it funds:** Community resilience, water quality, environmental education in underserved Gulf communities
- **Award size:** Approximately 5 grants of up to $6M each ($30M total from Bipartisan Infrastructure Law)
- **Status:** Active
- **Requirements:** Must serve underserved communities in Gulf states (TX, LA, MS, AL, FL); institutions of higher learning are eligible
- **CECREH fit:** Good. Community resilience technology for underserved Texas Gulf Coast communities aligns. Large award size. Texas Tech's eligibility as an institution of higher learning is a plus.

### Meadows Foundation

- **What it funds:** Civic and public affairs, human services, education, environment, health -- all in Texas
- **Award size:** Varies widely ($25K to multi-million)
- **Status:** Active (new grant portal launched August 2025)
- **Requirements:** Must be 501(c)(3) or public entity; must benefit Texas
- **CECREH fit:** Good under civic and public affairs or human services. Flexible Texas-only funder. The qualitative renter vulnerability research and focus on elderly/disabled populations aligns with their human services emphasis. Texas Tech qualifies as a public entity.

### Robert Wood Johnson Foundation -- Community Resilience and Climate Health

- **What it funds:** Community resilience, climate health, health equity research
- **Award size:** $50K (Local Data for Equitable Communities) to $5M (major grants)
- **Status:** Active with multiple 2026 opportunities
- **Requirements:** Must advance health equity
- **CECREH fit:** Strong. The renter vulnerability research aligns with health equity priorities. "Local Data for Equitable Communities" ($50K, 2026) could fund the interview-based research component specifically. The housing-health nexus is well-documented and aligns with CECREH's mission.

### NOAA Climate Resilience Regional Challenge

- **What it funds:** Collaborative projects increasing coastal community resilience to extreme weather and climate change
- **Award size:** From a $575M total program
- **Status:** Active (awards being distributed)
- **Requirements:** Community engagement, regional coordination, capacity building
- **CECREH fit:** Strong. NWS integration directly connects to NOAA's mission. Coastal community resilience to hurricanes and flooding in Texas is a core use case. "Capacity building" is literally in CECREH's name.

### Rebuild Texas Fund / OneStar Foundation

- **What it funds:** Nonprofit/public-sector disaster recovery and long-term resilience
- **Award size:** Varies by disaster event
- **Status:** Active but no open funding round currently. Most recent: Central Texas Flood Recovery Fund (July 2025)
- **Requirements:** Must serve historically underserved populations
- **CECREH fit:** Strong thematic alignment with CECREH's focus on vulnerable populations and resilient housing. Subscribe to the newsletter and apply when the next round opens.

## Tier 3: Worth Exploring

### TDEM Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP)

- **What it funds:** Projects reducing future disaster losses, including innovative/non-structural measures
- **Award size:** Varies by disaster declaration (hundreds of millions statewide per cycle)
- **Status:** Active on rolling basis following Presidential disaster declarations
- **Requirements:** Tied to declared disasters; requires local hazard mitigation plan; 75/25 cost share
- **CECREH fit:** Moderate. The "innovative project" category could accommodate an AI guidance system. Traditionally infrastructure-oriented, but non-structural mitigation is expanding.

### Texas A&M Hazard Reduction and Recovery Center (HRRC)

- **What it funds:** $5.1M annual research portfolio in hazard reduction and recovery
- **Status:** Active
- **CECREH fit:** Excellent for the qualitative renter vulnerability research. HRRC has deep expertise in social science dimensions of disaster recovery and housing. Could be a collaborator or co-PI partner rather than a funder. The HRRC-CECREH partnership would strengthen proposals to any of the programs listed above.

### Texas A&M / TDEM Emergency Management Applied Research Grants

- **What it funds:** Applied research with direct value to emergency management practitioners
- **Award size:** Small (~$10K--$30K from a $165K total pool)
- **Status:** Active; awarded at annual TDEM symposium
- **CECREH fit:** An AI disaster guidance system is exactly "applied research for EM practitioners." Awards are small but provide legitimacy and connections to TDEM -- valuable for building the community partnership needed for larger grants (S&CC, CIVIC, BRIC).

### Moody Foundation

- **What it funds:** Social services, environment, children's issues (Galveston-based)
- **Award size:** Over $80M/year total
- **Status:** Active
- **CECREH fit:** Moderate. Gulf Coast relevance given Galveston headquarters. Has historically supported post-Harvey recovery work. The social services category could encompass housing resilience.

### EPA Environmental and Climate Justice Community Change Grants

- **What it funds:** Climate and environmental justice activities in disinvested communities
- **Award size:** From a $2B total program
- **Status:** Awards launching in 2025
- **CECREH fit:** Strong for the renter/tenant vulnerability component in Gulf Coast environmental justice communities. The housing-environment-justice intersection is central to CECREH's work.

### NIEHS Time-Sensitive Research (R21)

- **What it funds:** Health studies during/following disasters
- **Award size:** Up to $275K over 2 years
- **Status:** Active through FY2026
- **Requirements:** Must be triggered by a specific disaster event; rapid 90--120 day review
- **CECREH fit:** Narrow but relevant. Have the application pre-written for the next Texas disaster event. NIEHS deployed CASPER at Texas A&M during July 2025 flooding -- an existing relationship to leverage. Housing health impacts post-disaster are well within scope.
