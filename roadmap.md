# Multi-Proposal Roadmap for CECREH

A phased strategy for pursuing multiple grants in parallel without scope overlap.

---

## Guiding Principle: Distinct Scientific Scope

NSF allows (and encourages) related proposals to different programs as long as each has a **distinct intellectual contribution**. Each proposal must stand alone scientifically -- they can share the same platform/testbed but must ask different research questions.

---

## Scope Partitioning

| Proposal | Core Research Question | Project Components | Intellectual Contribution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ReDDDoT | How should AI disaster systems be designed, governed, and deployed responsibly? | AI Disaster Agent: governance framework, authority-grounding, transparency | Responsible AI design principles for safety-critical public systems |
| S&CC | How does community-deployed AI change emergency preparedness behaviors? | AI Disaster Agent (deployed), community partner data | Community-level behavioral and resilience impacts |
| HCC | How do individuals interact with and trust AI under crisis conditions? | AI Disaster Agent (experimental instrument), user studies | Fundamental HCI knowledge: trust, cognitive load, equity |
| Gulf Research | How can AI reduce health/housing disparities in disaster outcomes for Gulf communities? | Full project, Gulf Hazard Interview data | Evidence-based strategies for equitable disaster resilience |
| RAPID | What are real-time usage patterns during an active disaster? | AI Disaster Agent (field deployment), usage logs | Empirical data on AI-mediated information seeking |
| DRMS | How do housing tenure and socioeconomic factors shape disaster risk decisions? | Gulf Hazard Interview data, CENTAUR platform | Decision science insights on vulnerability and risk perception |

## Timeline

### Phase 0: Foundation (March--May 2026)

- [ ] **Secure community partner** -- Contact Harris County OEM, Galveston County EM, or Texas Division of Emergency Management. This is the single highest-leverage action -- it unlocks S&CC, CIVIC, BRIC, and strengthens every other proposal.
- [ ] **Texas A&M HRRC outreach** -- Reach out about collaboration on renter vulnerability research. Builds the partnership needed for larger grants.
- [ ] **Email NSF Program Officers** -- Send 1-page project summaries to POs at ReDDDoT and HCC. Ask: "Is this in scope? Which program is the best fit?"
- [ ] **IRB protocol** -- Begin IRB application for user studies with disaster-affected populations. Takes 2--4 months and gates all human subjects work.
- [ ] **Preliminary results package** -- Prepare Gulf Hazard Interview findings as preliminary data (works for all proposals).
- [ ] **GLO Coastal Management Cycle 32** -- Evaluate and prepare application (due June 3, 2026).

### Phase 1: Planning Grants (Summer--Fall 2026)

**Submission 1 -- ReDDDoT Phase 1 Planning Grant ($300K/2yr)**

- Target: Next open window (check NSF 25-536 for dates)
- Scope: Responsible design framework for AI disaster systems -- governance, transparency, authority-grounding
- Team: CECREH PI + responsible AI co-PI + community/government partner
- Deliverable: Design principles, pilot evaluation, Phase 2 readiness

**Submission 2 -- S&CC-DG Planning Grant ($150K/1yr)**

- Target: Fall 2026 window (~September--November)
- Scope: Planning for community deployment -- needs assessment, partnership formalization, pilot design
- Team: CECREH PI + community partner (county EM office)
- Deliverable: Community needs assessment, deployment plan, IRG proposal readiness

**Submission 3 -- Gulf Research Program (if call opens)**

- Target: Next open call
- Scope: Reducing health and housing disparities in disaster outcomes for Gulf Coast communities
- Team: CECREH PI + public health co-PI + Gulf Coast community partners
- Deliverable: Evidence base for equitable AI-mediated disaster guidance

These can be submitted in the same cycle because the scopes are distinct: ReDDDoT focuses on responsible AI design, S&CC focuses on community partnership and deployment, and Gulf Research focuses on health/housing equity outcomes.

### Phase 2: Full Proposals (2027)

**Submission 4 -- HCC / Future CoRe (~$800K--1M/4yr)**

- Target: February or September 2027
- Scope: Fundamental HCI research -- trust calibration in crisis AI, cognitive load adaptation, equity in human-AI interaction
- Prerequisites: Preliminary user study data from planning grants; IRB approval
- This is the "big science" proposal -- use planning grant results as preliminary data

**Submission 5 -- S&CC-IRG ($1.5M/3--4yr)** (if SCC-DG was funded)

- Target: Spring 2027 (preliminary proposal), then full proposal
- Scope: Full community deployment and longitudinal impact study
- Prerequisites: SCC-DG results, formalized community partnership

### Phase 3: Opportunistic (Ongoing)

**RAPID ($200K)** -- Submit within days of a major Gulf Coast weather event

- Have the proposal 90% pre-written and ready to submit on short notice
- Data collected feeds into HCC and S&CC proposals as preliminary results

**CIVIC Stage 1 ($75K/6mo)** -- Submit when CIVIC 4.0 round opens

- Good fallback if S&CC-DG is not funded

**FEMA BRIC** -- Submit when NOFO reopens

- Through TDEM; capability-building category
- Potentially the largest single award ($1M--$5M)

**TDEM Applied Research Grants** -- Annual cycle

- Small ($10K--$30K) but builds TDEM relationships for HMGP and BRIC

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| No community partner found | Pursue HCC and DRMS first (no partner required); use Gulf Hazard Interview contacts to build relationships |
| Planning grant not funded | Use reviewer feedback to strengthen and resubmit. Submit HCC in parallel (no planning grant needed) |
| RAPID opportunity does not arise | Not critical -- focus on controlled user studies instead of field deployment |
| PO says "not in scope" | Redirect to the program they suggest. PO feedback is the most valuable pre-submission signal |
| Overlapping scope flagged by reviewers | Reference the scope partitioning matrix in Current and Pending documents |
| FEMA BRIC remains in legal limbo | Pursue NSF and foundation funding as primary track; BRIC is supplementary |
